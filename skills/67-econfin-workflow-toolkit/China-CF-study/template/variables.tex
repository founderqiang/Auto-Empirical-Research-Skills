\caption{\textbf{Variable Definitions}}
\label{definition}

\begin{tabular}{p{2.0cm}p{13.5cm}}
\toprule
Variable       &        Definition\\
\hline\\
Institution   & The ratio of the number of shares held by institutional investors to the total number of shares at the year-end.\\
PTC   & A dummy variable that equals one if the city where the firm is located has established a patent trading center (PTC) in or before year $t$, and zero otherwise.\\
Size   & Natural logarithm of total assets. \\
Balance   & Degree of equity balance, calculated as the sum of shareholdings of the 2nd to 10th largest shareholders divided by the shareholding of the largest shareholder. \\
ROA   & The ratio of net income to total assets. \\
Tobinq   & The ratio of the market value of assets to the book value of assets. \\
Age   & Natural logarithm of the number of years since the firm's establishment.\\
Cash   & Net cash flow from operating activities divided by total assets. \\
Indept   & The ratio of the number of independent directors to total board members. \\
Top1   & The shareholding ratio of the biggest shareholder. \\
SOE   & A dummy variable that equals one if the ultimate controller is the government or a state-owned enterprise, and zero otherwise. \\
Dual   & a dummy variable that equals one if the same individual concurrently holds the positions of chairman and general manager and zero otherwise. \\
FinDev   & City financial development, measured as the ratio of the balance of loans of financial institutions to regional GDP. \\
Innov   & City innovation potential, measured as the ratio of science and technology expenditure to local general budget expenditure. \\
Open   & City openness, measured as the ratio of actually utilized Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) to regional GDP. \\
Gov   & Government intervention, measured as the ratio of local general budget expenditure to regional GDP. \\
TechFin   & A dummy variable equal to one if the city is approved as a pilot zone for combining technology and finance, and zero otherwise. \\
Broadband   & a dummy variable that equals one if a city is approved as a “Broadband China” pilot city in a given year and thereafter, and zero otherwise. \\
Court   & A dummy variable equal to one if an intellectual property court or tribunal is established in the city, and zero otherwise. \\
JunkPatents   & Strategic innovators identified as firms with a non-invention patent application ratio greater than 90\%. \\
RealInnovation   & Substantive innovators identified as firms with a non-invention patent application ratio less than 50\%. \\
DebtPatience & Calculated as the ratio of relational debt to total liabilities. Relational debt aggregates Notes Payable, Long-term Loans, Bonds Payable, and Long-term Payables, representing formal financing sources secured through explicit credit contracts and long-term relationships rather than spontaneous operating activities. \\
EquityPatience   & Measured as the stability of institutional ownership, calculated as the ratio of the institutional shareholding percentage to its standard deviation over the past three years. \\
DeltaCash   & Annual change in cash and cash equivalents over total assets. \\
IntCoverage   & Earnings before interest and taxes divided by interest expense. \\
Lev   & The total leverage ratio, calculated as the ratio of total liabilities to total assets. \\
Maturity   & The debt maturity structure, calculated as the ratio of long-term loans to total liabilities. \\
LnLongloan   & The absolute volume of long-term credit, measured as the natural logarithm of one plus long-term loans. \\
LTLeverage   & The long-term debt leverage ratio, calculated as the ratio of long-term loans to total assets. \\
Cod   & The cost of debt financing, measured as the ratio of total financial expenses to total liabilities at the end of the fiscal year. \\
Tenure   & Institutional holding tenure, calculated as the average number of consecutive years that institutional investors have appeared in the top ten shareholders list. \\
AbsDA   & Accrual earnings management, measured as the absolute value of discretionary accruals estimated using the Modified Jones Model. \\
Perks   & Managerial perks, measured as the ratio of management expenses (minus the total compensation of directors, supervisors, and senior executives) to total operating revenue. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\toprule
PatentStock   & A dummy variable equal to one if the firm possesses granted patents, and zero otherwise. \\
ATO   & Asset turnover ratio (Revenue divided by total assets), used to measure operating efficiency. \\
Z-score   & Altman Z-score used to proxy for financial distress risk, calculated based on the firm's liquidity, profitability, leverage, and solvency. \\
Turnover   & Annual stock turnover rate, used to proxy for market speculation or turnover pressure. \\
MarketShare   & Market share, defined as firm revenue divided by total industry revenue. \\
TradingCapital   & Combined ownership of mutual funds, securities brokers, and trusts. \\
PatientCapital   & Combined ownership of QFIIs, insurance companies, and social security funds. \\
StrategicCapital   & Combined ownership of banks, finance companies, and non-financial corporate blockholders. \\
Investment   & The firm's investment rate, measured as cash paid for acquiring fixed assets and intangible assets divided by lagged total assets. \\
StrategicMix   & The strategic investment allocation ratio, defined as R\&D expenses divided by the sum of R\&D and capital expenditure. \\
LnInstitution   & The natural logarithm of one plus the total institutional shareholding ratio at the year-end. \\
ProfInst   & The combined shareholding ratio of mutual funds, securities brokers, and trust companies relative to floating A-shares. \\
Fund   & The shareholding ratio of mutual funds relative to floating A-shares. \\
Relaxation   & a dummy variable that equals one if the price limit on the firm’s trading board is relaxed, and zero otherwise. (ChiNext in 2020 and Sci-Tech innovation board in 2019). \\
PatentReform   & A dummy variable that equals one if the patent approval system reform is in the city where the firm is located, and zero otherwise. \\

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
