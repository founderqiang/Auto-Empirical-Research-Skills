# Journal House-Style Profiles

Title and section-structure conventions for common target journals. Each profile covers the same dimensions:

- **Title**: phrasing, case, subtitle/colon tolerance
- **Numbering**: section/subsection numbering scheme
- **Heading case**: sentence case vs. title case
- **Introduction**: numbered, unnumbered, or headingless
- **Skeleton**: the canonical empirical-paper section sequence
- **Literature review**: standalone section or folded into the introduction
- **Hypotheses**: formally numbered statements vs. predictions in prose
- **Section count**: typical range for the main body

These are *conventions extracted from recently published articles*, not submission-system requirements. When a paper's content genuinely doesn't fit the canonical skeleton, follow the journal's spirit (compactness, naming style, case) rather than forcing sections to exist.

## JFE

**Journal of Financial Economics** — Elsevier finance family.

- **Title**: short, declarative noun phrase in sentence-grade concision; "X and Y: Evidence from Z" subtitles acceptable; question titles rare. Creative short titles are fine if informative.
- **Numbering**: Arabic — `1.`, `2.`; subsections `2.1`, `2.1.1`.
- **Heading case**: **sentence case** ("2. Data and sample construction"). Keep proper nouns/acronyms capitalized.
- **Introduction**: numbered, "1. Introduction".
- **Skeleton**:
  1. Introduction (contribution + literature positioning folded in)
  2. Institutional background and/or hypotheses *(optional, short)*
  3. Data and sample *(or "Data and summary statistics")*
  4. Empirical design and main results *(may carry a descriptive name, e.g., "4. Ratings and fund flows")*
  5. Mechanisms / channels
  6. Robustness and additional analyses *(commonly "Additional analyses")*
  7. Conclusion
- **Literature review**: never standalone; folded into the introduction.
- **Hypotheses**: predictions in prose; formally numbered hypotheses are atypical — fold a "Hypothesis Development" section into a short background section or the introduction.
- **Section count**: 6–8. Ten or more narrative sections is a clear restructuring signal: merge dynamics/welfare/cross-validation material into "Additional analyses".
- **Avoid**: narrative section names ("How Issuers Respond to a Downgrade"), three-concept headings ("Robustness, the Platform Era, and Placebo Tests"), mechanism subsections styled "Channel 1 -- ..." (use plain descriptive subsection names).

## JF

**Journal of Finance** — Wiley.

- **Title**: Title Case, moderately short; occasional question titles.
- **Numbering**: Roman numerals — `I.`, `II.`; subsections lettered `A.`, `B.`; sub-subsections `A.1`.
- **Heading case**: **title case** ("II. Data and Methodology").
- **Introduction**: typically **headingless and unnumbered** — body text begins right after the abstract; the first numbered section is "I. ..." (often the data or framework section). Some papers do number "I. Introduction"; prefer the headingless convention when restructuring unless the user objects.
- **Skeleton**: [unnumbered introduction] → I. Hypotheses/Framework or Data → II. Empirical Methodology → III. Main Results → IV. Mechanisms/Additional Results → V. Conclusion.
- **Literature review**: folded into the introduction.
- **Hypotheses**: prose predictions; light formal numbering tolerated in a framework section.
- **Section count**: 5–7 Roman sections.
- **Note**: robustness material often lives in an **Internet Appendix**; keep the main body lean.

## RFS

**Review of Financial Studies** — OUP.

- **Title**: Title Case; question titles ("Do X ...?") are relatively common and acceptable.
- **Numbering**: Arabic — `1.`, `2.`; subsections `2.1`.
- **Heading case**: **title case** ("2. Data and Variable Construction").
- **Introduction**: numbered, "1. Introduction".
- **Skeleton**: similar to JFE — 1. Introduction; 2. (Background/Hypotheses); 3. Data; 4. Main results; 5. Mechanisms; 6. Robustness; 7. Conclusion.
- **Literature review**: folded into the introduction.
- **Hypotheses**: prose predictions; a short "Testable implications" subsection is acceptable.
- **Section count**: 6–8.

## JFQA

**Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis** — Cambridge.

- **Title**: Title Case, concise.
- **Numbering**: Roman numerals `I.`, `II.`; subsections `A.`, `B.` (JF-like).
- **Heading case**: title case.
- **Introduction**: numbered "I. Introduction".
- **Skeleton/lit review/hypotheses/count**: as JF, but the introduction carries a heading.

## Management Science

INFORMS.

- **Title**: declarative or question; colon subtitles common; somewhat longer titles tolerated than JFE/JF.
- **Numbering**: Arabic — `1.`, `2.`; subsections `2.1`.
- **Heading case**: **title case** ("2. Theory and Hypotheses").
- **Introduction**: numbered, "1. Introduction".
- **Skeleton**:
  1. Introduction
  2. Related Literature *and/or* Theory and Hypotheses *(standalone — this is the big difference from finance journals)*
  3. Institutional Setting *(if relevant)*
  4. Data and Empirical Strategy
  5. Results
  6. Mechanisms / Additional Analyses
  7. Discussion and Conclusion *(managerial/policy implications welcome)*
- **Literature review**: standalone section is standard and expected.
- **Hypotheses**: **formally stated and numbered** — "Hypothesis 1 (H1). ..." — developed in the theory section and referenced in results.
- **Section count**: 6–8.

## AER

**American Economic Review** — AEA family (also AEJ: Applied, AEJ: Policy, etc.).

- **Title**: short, declarative; restrained subtitles.
- **Numbering**: Roman numerals `I.`, `II.`; subsections `A.`, `B.`.
- **Heading case**: title case ("II. Theoretical Framework").
- **Introduction**: **headingless and unnumbered** — text begins directly; first heading is "I. ...".
- **Skeleton**: [introduction] → I. Background/Model → II. Data → III. Empirical Strategy → IV. Results → V. Conclusion.
- **Literature review**: folded into the introduction.
- **Hypotheses**: prose; model-derived predictions.
- **Section count**: 5–7.

## QJE

**Quarterly Journal of Economics** — OUP.

- **Title**: short, Title Case; QJE titles are famously punchy.
- **Numbering**: Roman numerals; subsections `I.A`, `I.B`.
- **Heading case**: title case (the journal typesets headings in caps; manuscripts use title case).
- **Introduction**: numbered, "I. Introduction".
- **Skeleton**: I. Introduction → II. Background/Model → III. Data → IV. Empirical Strategy and Results → V. Mechanisms → VI. Conclusion.
- **Literature review**: folded into the introduction.
- **Section count**: 5–7.

## RAND

**RAND Journal of Economics** — Wiley.

- **Title**: concise; question titles common.
- **Numbering**: Arabic `1.`, `2.`; subsections often short bold side-heads.
- **Heading case**: **sentence case** ("2. The model").
- **Introduction**: numbered "1. Introduction".
- **Skeleton**: theory-forward — 1. Introduction; 2. Institutional background; 3. Model *(or theoretical framework — if the paper has no model, a "Conceptual framework" section with testable predictions serves)*; 4. Data; 5. Empirical results; 6. Conclusion.
- **Literature review**: folded into the introduction.
- **Hypotheses**: model-derived predictions in prose.
- **Section count**: 5–7.
- **Caution**: if the paper lacks any theoretical framing, flag it to the user — RAND readers expect one; do not fabricate it.

## JIE

**Journal of International Economics** — Elsevier.

- **Title**: sentence-style concision; "Evidence from" subtitles common.
- **Numbering**: Arabic; subsections `2.1`.
- **Heading case**: **sentence case**.
- **Introduction**: numbered "1. Introduction".
- **Skeleton**: 1. Introduction; 2. (Stylized facts / Measurement — common in JIE); 3. (Model — optional); 4. Data; 5. Empirical results; 6. Robustness; 7. Conclusion.
- **Literature review**: folded into the introduction.
- **Section count**: 6–8.

## Research Policy

Elsevier innovation/management.

- **Title**: longer descriptive titles and colon subtitles are normal; questions acceptable.
- **Numbering**: Arabic; subsections `2.1`.
- **Heading case**: **sentence case** ("2. Literature review and hypotheses").
- **Introduction**: numbered "1. Introduction".
- **Skeleton**:
  1. Introduction
  2. Literature review and hypotheses *(standalone, with numbered H1/H2...)*
  3. Institutional background *(for policy/China papers)*
  4. Data and methods
  5. Results
  6. Discussion *(theoretical contributions, policy implications, limitations — often subsections)*
  7. Conclusion *(or "6. Discussion and conclusions" combined)*
- **Literature review**: standalone and expected.
- **Hypotheses**: formally numbered (H1, H2, ...).
- **Section count**: 6–7.

## JCF / JBF

**Journal of Corporate Finance / Journal of Banking & Finance** — Elsevier.

- Same mechanics as JFE: Arabic numbering, **sentence case**, numbered "1. Introduction".
- **Difference from JFE**: a short standalone "2. Literature and hypothesis development" section is tolerated and common; formally numbered hypotheses (H1/H2) acceptable.
- **Skeleton**: 1. Introduction; 2. Literature and hypotheses; 3. Data and methodology; 4. Empirical results; 5. Robustness/additional tests; 6. Conclusion.
- **Section count**: 6–7.

## Accounting

**JAR / TAR / JAE.**

- **Numbering**: Arabic. **Heading case**: JAE (Elsevier) sentence case; JAR and TAR title case (TAR typesets small caps).
- **Introduction**: numbered "1. Introduction" (JAR often Roman "1." styling; keep Arabic in manuscript).
- **Skeleton**: 1. Introduction; 2. Background and hypothesis development *(standalone — standard in accounting)*; 3. Research design and data; 4. Results; 5. Additional analyses; 6. Conclusion.
- **Hypotheses**: formally numbered (H1, H2), often stated in italics.
- **Section count**: 6–7.

## Family Mapping

For a journal not profiled above, map by publisher/field and confirm with the user:

| If the journal is... | Use profile | Adjust |
|---|---|---|
| Elsevier finance/economics (e.g., JFI, JEBO, JEDC, China Economic Review, PBFJ) | JFE | lit-review tolerance is higher outside the top 3 |
| Wiley/OUP/Chicago top-5 economics (JPE, ReStud, Econometrica) | AER | Econometrica: numbered "1. Introduction", heavier model sections |
| AEA journals (AEJ family) | AER | — |
| INFORMS (Org Science, M&SOM) | Management Science | — |
| Management/strategy (SMJ, AMJ, JMS) | Management Science | hypothesis development even more central |
| Field finance (RF, FM, JFS) | RFS | — |
| Innovation/policy (JPIM, Technovation, JTT) | Research Policy | — |

When even the family is unclear, fetch the latest issue's table of contents and skim 2–3 recent articles for: title phrasing, numbering scheme, heading case, lit-review placement, and section count. Summarize the derived profile to the user before applying it.
