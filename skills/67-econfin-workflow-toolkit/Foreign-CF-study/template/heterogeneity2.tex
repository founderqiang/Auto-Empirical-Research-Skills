\caption{\textbf{Heterogeneity Conditional on Market Risk and Competitive Position}}
\label{heterogeneity2}

\noindent{\justifying{This table presents the heterogeneous association between local patent trading infrastructure and institutional ownership conditional on corporate market risk and competitive position for a sample of Chinese A-share listed firms from 2003 to 2023. Firms are divided into subsamples based on the annual median values of Financial Distress (measured by \textsl{Z-score}), Share Turnover (measured by \textsl{Turnover}), and Market Position (measured by \textsl{MarketShare}). Specifically,\textsl{Z-score} proxies for financial risk based on liquidity and solvency; \textsl{Turnover} proxies for market speculation; and \textsl{MarketShare} is defined as firm revenue relative to total industry revenue. The dependent variable is \textsl{Institution}, defined as the ratio of shares held by institutional investors to total shares at year-end. The main explanatory variable is \textsl{PTC} (a dummy variable that equals one if the city where the firm is located has established a patent trading center in or before year $t$, and zero otherwise). The set of control variables is consistent with that of the baseline regression. All regressions include firm, year, and city fixed effects. The robust $t$-statistics clustered by the firm are reported in parentheses. The last row reports the empirical $p$-value for the difference in the coefficients of \textsl{PTC} between the high and low groups, computed via a bootstrap Fisher permutation test with 500 replications. Detailed variable definitions are presented in Table \ref{definition}. ***, **, and * denote the significance at the 1\%, 5\%, and 10\% levels, respectively.\par}}
