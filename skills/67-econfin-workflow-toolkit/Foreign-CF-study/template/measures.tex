\noindent{\justifying{This table presents the association between local patent trading infrastructure and institutional ownership for a sample of Chinese A-share listed firms from 2003 to 2023 using alternative variable definitions. The dependent variables are \textsl{LnInstitution} (the natural logarithm of one plus the total institutional shareholding ratio) in Columns (1)–(2), \textsl{ProfInst} (the combined shareholding ratio of mutual funds, securities brokers, and trusts) in Columns (3)–(4), and \textsl{Fund} (the shareholding ratio of mutual funds) in Columns (5)–(6). The main explanatory variable is \textsl{PTC}, a dummy variable that equals 1 if the city where the firm is located has established a patent trading center by year $t$, and 0 otherwise. Other control variables include \textsl{Size} (the natural logarithm of total assets), \textsl{Balance} (the degree of equity balance), \textsl{ROA} (the ratio of net income to total assets), \textsl{Tobinq} (the market-to-book ratio), \textsl{Age} (the natural logarithm of firm age), \textsl{Cash} (net cash flow divided by total assets), \textsl{Indept} (the percentage of independent directors), \textsl{Top1} (the shareholding ratio of the largest shareholder), \textsl{Dual} (a dummy variable for chairman and CEO duality), \textsl{FinDev} (city financial development), \textsl{Innov} (city innovation potential), \textsl{Open} (city openness), and \textsl{Gov} (government intervention). All regressions include firm and city fixed effects, alongside year or industry $\times$ year fixed effects. The robust $t$-statistics clustered by the firm are reported in parentheses. The detailed variable definitions are presented in Table S1. ***, **, and * denote the significance at the 1\%, 5\%, and 10\% levels, respectively.\par}}
