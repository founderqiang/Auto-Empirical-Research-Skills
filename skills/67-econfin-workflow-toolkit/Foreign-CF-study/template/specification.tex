\noindent{\justifying{This table reports the robustness check results using high-dimensional interactive fixed effects for a sample of Chinese A-share listed firms from 2003 to 2023. The dependent variable is \textsl{Institution}, defined as the ratio of the number of shares held by institutional investors to the total number of shares at the year-end. The main explanatory variable is \textsl{PTC} (a dummy variable that equals one if the city where the firm is located has established a patent trading center in or before year $t$, and zero otherwise). Control variables include \textsl{Size} (natural logarithm of total assets), \textsl{Balance} (degree of equity balance, calculated as the sum of shareholdings of the 2nd to 10th largest shareholders divided by the shareholding of the largest shareholder), \textsl{ROA} (the ratio of net income to total assets), \textsl{Tobinq} (the ratio of the market value of assets to the book value of assets), \textsl{Age} (natural logarithm of the number of years since the firm's establishment), \textsl{Cash} (net cash flow from operating activities divided by total assets), \textsl{Indept} (the ratio of the number of independent directors to total board members), \textsl{Top1} (the shareholding ratio of the biggest shareholder), \textsl{Dual} (a dummy variable that equals one if the same individual concurrently holds the positions of chairman and general manager and zero otherwise), \textsl{FinDev} (city financial development, measured as the ratio of the balance of loans of financial institutions to regional GDP), \textsl{Innov} (city innovation potential, measured as the ratio of science and technology expenditure to local general budget expenditure), \textsl{Open} (city openness, measured as the ratio of actually utilized Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) to regional GDP), and \textsl{Gov} (government intervention, measured as the ratio of local general budget expenditure to regional GDP). All regressions include firm, year, and city fixed effects. Column (1) further controls for industry $\times$ year fixed effects. Column (2) further controls for province $\times$ year fixed effects. Column (3) simultaneously controls for both industry $\times$ year and province $\times$ year fixed effects. The robust $t$-statistics clustered by the firm are reported in parentheses. ***, **, and * denote the significance at the 1\%, 5\%, and 10\% levels, respectively.\par}}
